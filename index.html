What is EMDR?
( Eye Movement Desensitization Reprocessing )
EMDR is a groundbreaking therapy used by therapists to help their clients heal after traumatic life experiences. It has been used to treat conditions such as PTSD and OCD as well as depression, panic, and anxiety disorders. During the therapy, a professional helps to guide patients through the recoding of traumatic memories. This allows patients to remove psychological blocks to healing in a faster time frame than traditional talk therapy. Visual bilateral stimulation is thought to trigger biological mechanisms linked to those activated during REM (rapid eye movement) sleep. A growing consensus of academic studies have shown that EMDR is a highly effective therapy for many trauma patients.
